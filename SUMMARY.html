End-of-Turn Detection for a Live Voice Agent

Plivo ML assignment · roll 24BT10010 · STT track · all scores are out-of-fold (models never scored a turn they trained on)

The problem, and why the status quo loses 1.6 seconds

A voice agent must decide at every pause: is the caller done, or just thinking? The baseline every production VAD uses — wait for N ms of silence — needs N ≈ 1600 ms on the English data to keep interruptions under 5%, because mid-turn hesitations routinely last a second or more (English hold pauses: p95 = 1.8 s). Every one of those 1600 ms is dead air the caller hears after they finished speaking. Our model reads how the speech stopped, not just that it stopped, and takes the floor in a fraction of that.

How it works

  • Anchor at the true speech offset. The labels' pause_start trails the acoustic offset by a ~110 ms VAD hangover (we measured it: the last 100 ms before pause_start sit ~44 dB below speech). All windows end at the detected offset.
  • Acoustic model. A ~90k-parameter CNN over 2 s of 48-band log-mel plus explicit F0-semitone and voicing channels (mel smears pitch; the terminal fall needs a track), ending at the offset. Augmentation: end-jitter, gain, mel noise, time/freq masks. 2 seeds × 5 folds = 10 snapshots averaged.
  • Mined hard negatives. Every unannotated intra-speech silence <100 ms is a guaranteed continuation — the caller kept talking. We mine ~880 of them (≈2.8× more negatives) and train the CNN to keep quiet there. This targets exactly the false-cutoff acoustics the metric punishes.
  • Turn-structure model. GBM + logistic regression on 42 causal scalars: offset shape (energy fall-time, trailing-tail level/centroid/voicing), pitch finality (loudness-gated F0 slope/level/percentile, creak), hesitation cues (flat-pitch final runs), and turn context (elapsed time, pause index, prior-pause durations, speech fraction).
  • Ensemble. Probability average, weight 0.55 on the CNN — chosen on out-of-fold predictions with a 0.3·EN + 0.7·HI objective, because the hidden test is announced as mostly Hindi. The tabular models also use deployment-aligned cost weights: a hold only causes a cutoff if it outlasts the action delay, so long holds carry the training cost — same AUC, but errors migrate to harmless short holds (−34 ms EN, −49 ms HI).

What the model actually keys on (and where it plateaus)

Plotting turns showed the discriminative pattern directly: holds cut off sharply — energy drops from full speech level straight into the floor mid-phrase — while true ends decay gradually, often with a trailing breath or an unstressed final syllable, and a pitch fall into the offset. Hindi hesitations are shorter and more often filled (flat-pitch vowel), which is why a plain silence timer already does 850 ms there and why the pitch-flatness and offset-shape features carry more of the Hindi signal. The remaining errors are mostly semantic: utterances that are prosodically final but syntactically incomplete (and vice versa) — separating those needs lexical content, which the rules (correctly) deny us: no ASR, no pretrained models. That is the plateau.

Honest validation

  • Every reported score is out-of-fold (5-fold GroupKFold by turn) — no score in this report comes from a model scoring its own training turns.
  • Cross-language stress test (hidden set is mostly Hindi): train-English→score-Hindi AUC 0.685 for the tabular models — the features are turn-relative (semitones vs the caller's own median, dB vs the caller's own speech level) precisely so they transfer; the shipped model trains on both languages pooled.
  • predict.py was smoke-tested on a fresh folder copy at a different path (incl. a variant with label/pause_end columns stripped) and on five pathological folders: a pause 0.3 s into the file, a wav shorter than the 2 s window, scrambled row order, a missing wav (row falls back to the class prior instead of crashing), and unknown extra columns — all produce complete predictions.
  • Causality: every audio frame used ends at or before pause_start; the current pause's duration and label are never read. Enforced in one place per model (features_eot.pause_features, train_cnn.make_window).
